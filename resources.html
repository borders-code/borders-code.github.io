Resources

By Itzel Medina
August 22, 2019

Here is a list of available resources:

  1. Refuge --- Catholic Charitiy helps undocumented immigrants and refugees to legally find their way back to their family. Pass citeznship exams, and not get caught or exploited, as well as helping those who are undocumented work in the United States.

  2. Sanctuary--- The AFSC which stands for the American Friends Service Committee is a part of the immigrant Rights Program. They inform people on sanctuary places where undocumented immigrants will not be detained by ICE officers or immigration services.

  3. Card--- IDNYC allows undocumented immigrants to get an ID without a social security. It only requires a home address. They allow you to make an application with someone that will need a guardian to accompany them while they get the ID, and offers an online application that you could fill out as well. Anyone between the ages of 10-12 will need a guardian to accompany them while they get their ID, but 13 and up can apply for an ID themselves. The minimum age to get an ID is 10 years old.

  4. Additional Resources Combined --- This page provides a variety of information that could be used, including places to get an education, where you can recieve free health insurance and much more.

  5. Online Resources --- This website is a library that provides Hotlines PDF/Guides and more online tools that will help to educate families on their rights, healthcare, and much more.

  6. Community --- This website provides a varitey of resources gathered by your fellow community to help provide you with updated information.
